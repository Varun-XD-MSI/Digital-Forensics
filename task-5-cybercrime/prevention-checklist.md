# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist  
*(For College Students in India)*

## ✅ General Safety Tips

1. **Use strong and unique passwords**  
   Create passwords with a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using the same password across multiple apps or websites.

2. **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)**  
   Turn on 2FA for important accounts like email, banking apps, and social media for an extra layer of security.

3. **Do not share OTPs or passwords with anyone**  
   No bank, app, or official will ever ask for your OTP. Sharing it can lead to instant account compromise.

4. **Be cautious of phishing links and messages**  
   Avoid clicking on suspicious links received via SMS, email, or WhatsApp claiming prizes, job offers, or urgent account issues.

5. **Download apps only from official stores**  
   Install apps only from Google Play Store or Apple App Store. Avoid APK files from unknown sources.

6. **Secure your devices with lock and updates**  
   Use screen locks (PIN, fingerprint, or face lock) and keep your phone and apps updated to prevent vulnerabilities.

7. **Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions**  
   Do not access banking or payment apps on public Wi-Fi networks without a secure connection.

8. **Verify online sellers and job offers**  
   Always check reviews and authenticity before making payments or sharing personal details.

---

## 💳 UPI & Financial Safety Tips

9. **Double-check receiver details before payment**  
   Always confirm the UPI ID or name before sending money. Once sent, transactions are hard to reverse.

10. **Never approve unknown collect requests**  
    Do not approve “Request Money” notifications from unknown users. Scammers often trick users into paying instead of receiving money.

11. **Regularly monitor bank and UPI transaction history**  
    Check your statements frequently to spot any unauthorized transactions early.

---

## 🚨 Reporting Cybercrime

- **National Cyber Crime Portal:** https://cybercrime.gov.in  
- **Helpline Number:** 1930  

Report incidents immediately to increase the chances of recovering lost funds and preventing further fraud.
